Rules — Generate
Length (structure only)

Short → 1 short paragraph or 2–3 sentences. Just the core message, no padding.
Medium → 2–3 body paragraphs. Use 65–160 body words when the idea supplies enough
substance, or 35–80 for a sparse idea with no reason/details.
These are approximate targets, not hard minimums. A complete draft may be shorter;
reject it only when the request itself is missing or substantially incomplete.
Long → 5+ body paragraphs. Use 220+ words when the idea supplies enough facts.
For a sparse idea with no reason/details, use a clearly longer 55–100 word draft
instead of inventing facts, reasons, dates, or repetitive filler.
Five paragraphs is an approximate target: accept a complete, grounded four-paragraph
draft instead of padding or rejecting it solely for missing one paragraph.
Length must never change vocabulary difficulty or tone — a "long, casual, simple" output should still use simple words, just more of them.

Specificity → every body must restate and develop the actual subject of the idea.
Never replace the subject with generic wording about "the request," a "standard
process," unspecified "conditions," or repeated "let me know" sentences.

Lists → when content naturally has 3 or more distinct requirements, blockers,
questions, or similar items, put each item on its own numbered or bulleted line.
Never cram several numbered items or dashes into one paragraph. Ordinary prose
that is not a real list stays in paragraph form.
Inspect the idea before drafting: if the idea itself names 3+ items, tasks, costs,
or requirements, line breaks are mandatory. Never merge them with "additionally,"
"also," "firstly," or "secondly."

Tone (voice only)

Formal → professional language, no contractions, no slang, structured sentences.
Friendly → warm, approachable, contractions okay, personable phrasing.
Casual → relaxed, conversational, contractions and informal phrasing okay.
Tone must never change how long the output is or how advanced the vocabulary is — "formal" doesn't mean longer, "casual" doesn't mean simpler words.

Complexity (wording and sentence development)

Simple → short, plain, everyday words and short sentences.
Example: "Can you send me an update on the contract?"
Standard → normal, clear wording.
Example: "Could you share the current status of the contract?"
Advanced → more precise/formal words and more developed sentence structure, without
becoming stiff or bloated.
Example: "Could you provide an update on where things currently stand with the contract?"
Content and meaning stay identical. Individual sentence length may vary, but paragraph
count and total body word count must remain inside the selected Length range.

Independence rule (core constraint)

Length, Tone, and Complexity are three separate prompt instructions to the model, applied together but never merged into one instruction.
Changing one setting while holding the other two fixed must only change the dimension it controls. Example: Short + Formal + Advanced vs. Long + Formal + Advanced should differ only in amount of text, not in formality or word choice.

Context fields

Personal profile info (comma-separated key-value pairs) → auto-injected into the prompt so generated text can reference name, role, or other saved details without the user typing them.
Permanent Note → additional standing instruction always included in every generation (e.g. "always sign off as Eshan").
Writing-style notes are applied as instructions and never copied into the body.
Concrete factual notes (such as a referral number or date) are retained as facts.
Sign-off names belong only on the closing signature line, never in the greeting or body.
Both are optional — if empty, generation proceeds with generic phrasing, no errors.
Email signature → every generated email ends with the sign-off line, then the writer's full name on the next line when a name is saved. If no name is saved and no permanent note supplies one, use exactly "[Your Name]" on the final line. This is the only allowed bracketed placeholder; brackets never appear in the subject, greeting, or body.

Date fidelity

Never complete a partial date. Preserve only the month, day, year, time, and duration
components explicitly supplied by the idea or a factual note.

Persistence

All settings (Length, Tone, Complexity, profile, note) are saved in the popup and auto-apply to every future generation without re-entry.
